Timeline

Ideas become powerful when their history is visible

The same symbols students use today were built slowly: first to solve practical problems, then to create general methods, and finally to describe abstract mathematical structures.

1630s

Coordinates change geometry

Descartes and Fermat turn curves into equations and equations into shapes.

1670s

Calculus takes form

Newton and Leibniz develop methods for change, tangents, and accumulation.

1800s

Linear structure emerges

Matrices, determinants, vector spaces, and transformations become a shared language.

Calculus II

Why infinite series mattered

Series were not invented as classroom drills. They helped mathematicians approximate functions, predict motion, and make difficult quantities computable before digital tools existed.

Analytic Geometry I

The coordinate plane as a revolution

Coordinates made geometry symbolic. A curve could become an equation, and a geometric problem could be attacked with algebraic manipulation.

Linear Algebra

From determinants to matrices

Determinants appeared in the study of linear systems long before modern matrix notation. Later, matrices became objects worth studying for their own structure and transformations.
